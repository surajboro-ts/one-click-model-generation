# Toasts

### **Definition**

Toasts are used for confirmations and acknowledgments that require minimal user interaction. These event-driven messages appear by overlaying content at the bottom of the screen.

The alert is triggered **at the time of task completion** (at form submission or dismissing a dialog), animates from the bottom center of the browser window and stays on screen for **5 seconds** before its automatic dismissal. The user may choose to dismiss the toast alert early by tapping the close (X) icon button.

### **Usage**

- Use to confirm actions not clearly visible in the UI.
- Include a close button.
- Toasts should not be used to confirm actions that are clearly visible in the UI, like inline editing.
- Avoid showing more than one alert at a time

![](https://radiant.thoughtspot.com/uploads/4g4HXEisVlCphIsXFQjcjQ.png)

---

### **Content guidelines**

- Keep the message concise
- Stick with the past tense: e.g Success! You added your pinboards.
- Tone can be a little informal or emotive e.g Well done! You’re ready to share your chart.
