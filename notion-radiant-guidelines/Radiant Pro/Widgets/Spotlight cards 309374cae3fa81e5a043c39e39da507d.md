# Spotlight cards

### **Definition**

Spotlights are smaller system activated pop ups that focus attention on a specific part of the UI like a button, icon or piece of content. They can be used individually or in sequence to guide users through tasks with multiple steps or to highlight multiple UI changes in a flow.

### **Spotlight card types**

Spotlight cards are activated by system events such as users landing on a page or interacting with a specific area within a page. Users can dismiss the card by clicking the “x” or a designated "close" button. In scenarios where there is no highlighted area on the screen, clicking outside the card can dismiss it.

### **Content guidelines**

Spotlight pop-ups focus attention on a specific part of the UI, like a button, icon, or piece of content. Spotlights can be used in sequence to guide users through tasks with multiple steps or to highlight multiple UI changes in a flow.

Spotlights can include a small image, illustration, or video thumbnail

**Writing spotlights**

- Spotlights can have a 1 – 2 word title but it’s not mandatory
- Use the UX writing pattern for longer body text
- 40 characters per line for a maximum of 4 lines
- For large amounts of text, separate into 4-line blocks for scannability
- Can use a more conversational tone when appropriate
