## **Anatomy**

An icon is a combination of geometrical figures and outline strokes that adheres to a set of fixed constraints.

![](https://radiant.thoughtspot.com/uploads/CQzCN9hiSbBdaHBTg21XHw.png)

| **Grid** | Radiant Icons fit within a designated 18x18px grid. The center 16x16px live area is defined by two overlapping rectangles, two circles, and two key lines intersecting by 45 degrees. |
| --- | --- |
| **Stroke**
 | Consistent 2px stroke with sharp caps. |
| **Shape**
 | Filled shapes  -- thicker than 2px --  can simultaneously have both sharp and rounded (1px) corners. |
| **Gap** 
 | The is a minimal 1px gap between a shape and a stroke or between two stroke segments. |

## **New icons**

### **Guidelines for new icons**

![](https://radiant.thoughtspot.com/uploads/JQZsrRYy_ZjpU07xFwwUxQ.png)

Icon content is limited to the 16 x 16px live area, with 1px of padding around the perimeter and 1px of padding surrounds the live area.

Before drafting a new icon in Figma, take the time to familiarize yourself with the look and feel of similar icons. Create multiple versions of your icon and A/B test them, selecting the one that most successfully conveys your meaning.

- Icons cannot be larger than 18px
- Use an 18 x 18px icon size grid to design, then scale down to 16px, 14px, 12px size to use.
- Icon content must remain inside of the **live area**, which is the region of an image that is unlikely to be hidden from view (such as when sidebars appear upon scrolling).
- If additional visual weight is needed, content may extend into the padding between the live area and the **trim area** (the complete size of a graphic). No parts of the icon should extend outside of the trim area.

### **Tips for designing icons**

- Design icons that are simple, modern, friendly and universal
- Give filled shapes less space than stroke areas to help achieve visual balance
- To create a filled shape, mix sharp and rounded (1px) corners
- Make sure icons look good in small and even extra small sizes
- Flatten icons before exporting
- Avoid complex shapes
- Don’t offset icons outside the live area
- Keeps stroke weights and rounded stroke caps consistent
- Avoid non-geometric or organic shapes
